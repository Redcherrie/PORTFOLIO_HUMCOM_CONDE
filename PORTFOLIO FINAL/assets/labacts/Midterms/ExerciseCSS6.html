Nutrition

Proper nutrition helps keep energy levels up and protects against many age-related illnesses and diseases like heart disease, cancer, and diabetes. But how do you maintain an eating routine and diet that keeps you and your family healthy and works within your lifestyle and budget?

Karen Faith L. Conde



The foods you eat supply the nutrients your body needs to maintain your brain, muscle, bone, nerves, skin, blood circulation, and immune system, playing a crucial role in supporting all essential bodily functions and ensuring that each system works efficiently to promote overall health and well-being, allowing your body to perform both daily tasks and complex activities with energy and strength while also contributing to long-term vitality and resilience against diseases.

"Dietary supplement" is an umbrella term that includes individual vitamins, such as A, B, C, D, E, and K, as well as essential minerals like calcium, magnesium, and potassium, which are critical in supporting a wide range of bodily functions, from bone health and immune system strength to nerve function and energy production, ensuring the body has the necessary tools to maintain balance, repair tissues, and combat deficiencies, ultimately contributing to optimal physical and mental performance.



Even though supplements are popular, there is limited evidence they offer significant health benefits or help protect against disease, as many studies suggest that while they can provide nutritional support, they often cannot replace a balanced diet and lifestyle, and their effectiveness in preventing or treating various illnesses remains uncertain, with most health benefits being anecdotal rather than scientifically proven, leading experts to emphasize the importance of whole foods and proper medical care over relying solely on supplements.



Healthy eating doesn't have to be hard, as with a bit of preparation and planning, it can be easy to create healthy meals for the entire week, allowing you to save time, reduce stress, and ensure you have nutritious options readily available, all while avoiding the temptation of fast food or unhealthy snacks, making it simpler to stay on track with your health goals and maintain energy levels throughout the week.

Pick a specific day of the week to organize your meal prep routine efficiently, as this allows you to dedicate focused time to plan, shop for, and prepare meals in advance, ensuring that you can easily access healthy options throughout the week, saving time on busy days while also reducing the chances of making impulsive food choices or relying on less nutritious alternatives.
